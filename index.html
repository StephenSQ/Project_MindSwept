A SIMPLE GAME

Ah yes, the Minesweeper. What a classic retro video game. A simple puzzle game that simulates a risk-free minefield clearing experience. Once you've mastered it I bet you can clear out the minefield in the western deserts of Egypt in no time ;)

Compared to modern video games most people will definitely find it rather boring. But if you're a noob programmer maybe you've thought of trying to make this game since it seems simple, isn't it ? At least I do. At first I thought it's gonna be impossible for me to pull it off with my very limited knowledge on CS, but then 2D arrays are a thing :() - so maybe that's how I'm gonna make this game.

And so begins my journey to remake a mindfield clearing Sim... Well, it took me a week of mindnumbing work to code it in C. With enough logic you can find a way to make the game with 2D arrays as the grid, just picture it as a grid of tiles, and each tile has a custom data structure that holds values and the state of the tile in the grid - it's that easy :) *dies*

So here I am remaking it again as a mini web game in CodePen with help from fellow programmer @MattXA25. At first it was supposed to be a final project for school :D but I submited mine a semester after the deadline. Anyway, here's the result ;)

HOW TO SWEEP MINES

Here's a brief explanation on how to play the premium mine sweeping simulator.

The MineField is the 15 × 15 grid. It is divided into 225 tiles, up to 24% of which can have a mine underneath it. It is YOUR job to mark out all the tiles that're safe to step on. There are around 18 million mines left to be cleared here in total so we'll need your help to clear out the 225 m² areas that we've assigned you.

In order to know which tiles have mines inside, you'll need to pay attention to the NUMBER in the tiles. These numbers tell how many mines are there within a one tile radius of its vicinity, in other words they are radar data that show the number of mines within a 3 × 3 area around it.

For Example, if a tile has the number 3, there are 3 mines around a one tile radius around it. If you've uncovered the 5 tiles around it, including the (middle) tile itself that makes 6 tiles uncovered. With that you'll know that those 6 tiles are safe and since this middle tile with a three has a radar range of 3 × 3 tiles, that means the remaining 3 still covered tiles have got to be mines.

PS: There's a twist in this game, some things are different from the original minesweeper game. You'll figure it out by yourself ;) afterall humans are the most intelligent organism known to man.

Now, I hope you can undestand text instructions without visual illustrations and get the job done. !!! GOOD LUCK !!!

The [ DEV LOG ]

Here's a brief description about the game's development. *actually its not a devlog anymore but idc devlog sounds nice*

It Hard. Making this sure took a while, around a week of time was spent in total on developing all this in the span of about a month. Starting from somewhere in mid of November(I forgor) until 30th December where the JS for the game is finally finished - but I'll have revisits and revisions sometime later.

There's still quite a few flaws in the code design, one of which is my lack of knowledge in "unobtrusive web design" where I should've tried my best to NOT put other language's code(either CSS/JS) in the HTML. Initially, I thought it's better to put short stylings for specific elements directly in the HTML element rather than spending a few more lines of code in the CSS but generally most professionals prefer not to to keep their HMTL lookin' neat. Another thing is the use of onclick attributes which apparently breaks the rules of unobtrusive web design. I should've mastered how to use .addEventListener() instead so I don't need onclick's containing JS functions but shoot I learned this mid way of finishing the JS from someone's answer in StackOverflow made 11 years ago :/ Just next time ig.
